# Market structure basics for daily-chart swing trading (sample source)

Notes from a primer article, seeded to exercise the vault pipeline.

Price on daily charts tends to move in alternating swings: sequences of
higher highs and higher lows (uptrend structure) or lower highs and lower
lows (downtrend structure). A "market structure shift" occurs when price
decisively breaks the most recent swing point against the prevailing
sequence — e.g., after a series of lower highs, price closes above the last
lower high. Practitioners treat the break as more meaningful when it comes
with "displacement": a wide-range candle closing well beyond the level
rather than a marginal poke.

The claimed rationale is positioning: traders anchored to the old trend are
forced to exit when structure breaks, and their exits fuel continuation.
Displacement is read as evidence that larger participants, not noise,
drove the break.

The primer cautions that raw structure breaks fire constantly on lower
timeframes; daily-chart breaks with displacement are rarer and, per the
author, worth testing as multi-day continuation signals rather than as
precise entries.
